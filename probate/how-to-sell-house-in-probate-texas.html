How to Sell a House in Probate in Texas

When a loved one passes away and real estate is part of the estate, one of the most common questions families ask is how to sell a house during probate.

On this page
  1. Determine the executor's authority
  2. Secure the property
  3. Determine property value
  4. Decide how to sell

Determine the executor's authority

The executor must have legal authority to manage the property before major sale decisions are made. Families often benefit from confirming timing, authority, and next steps with the probate attorney early in the process.

Secure the property

Once a property is vacant, practical details matter quickly. Changing locks, maintaining utilities, and keeping insurance active can help protect the home while the estate decides what comes next.

Determine property value

Most families begin by getting a probate property valuation. A current market opinion helps the estate compare options, set expectations, and decide whether selling as-is or making light improvements makes more sense.

Decide how to sell

There are usually a few practical paths forward depending on condition, timeline, and family goals.

  • Sell as-is for speed and simplicity
  • Make light improvements before listing
  • Transfer the property to an heir if that better fits the estate plan
